Newton Programming Competition

The Newton Programming Competition is a 2-hour-long online coding competition organized by a group of high school students in Newton. Participants will be asked to develop their very own algorithms, think outside of the box, and solve interesting programming problems!

Newton Programming Competition first started in 2022 summer. This year, NPC is coming back in 2023! The 2023 Fall contest is going to be held on Sept 23rd, Saturday! We invited Dr. Brian Dean, the coach of IOI US National Team to give a talk!

About

  • Who We Are

    We are a group of high school students from Newton South High School, Newton MA. We both love Computer Science and are passionate to spread awareness and promote education about it!

  • Why NPC

    Many high schools do not offer a good variety of Computer Science courses either due to lack of interests, faculties, or budgets. Even some do, the content in the course may solely focus on the implementation sides of programming. NPC aims to create opportunities and open a new window for such young programmers.

  • What's Special

    Unlike Hackathons, NPC focuses on promoting programmers' problem-solving skills instead of implmentation skills. We make sure all participates are at the same level so that each individual can compete equally, challenge themselves, and stay motivated to learn!
